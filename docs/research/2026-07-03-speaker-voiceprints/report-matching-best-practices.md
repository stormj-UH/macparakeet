# Speaker-Verification Engineering Brief

For a local-first meeting app, treat speaker naming as open-set speaker identification on top of diarization: every diarized cluster may be a known person or may be unknown. The safest pattern is to score a clean, sufficiently long speaker embedding against enrolled profiles, then require both an absolute score threshold and a separation margin before auto-naming.

1. Threshold selection. Raw cosine thresholds do not travel well across embedding models, score transforms, microphones, rooms, codecs, languages, or speech durations. pyannote's public voiceprint API exposes a confidence threshold, not a universal cosine cutoff; its docs suggest higher values such as 50-70 for stricter matching and explicitly warn that a voiceprint may match even when the person is absent (https://docs.pyannote.ai/tutorials/identification-with-voiceprints, https://docs.pyannote.ai/tutorials/confidence-scores). For local cosine scoring, tune on same-app validation data and pick the operating point from ROC/DET tradeoffs. As a starting grid for L2-normalized wespeaker/Titanet/ECAPA-style embeddings, evaluate tau = 0.65/0.70/0.75/0.80 and launch conservatively, not globally. Evidence for fragility: pyannote's embedding model reports 2.8% EER on VoxCeleb using cosine distance without VAD/PLDA (https://huggingface.co/pyannote/embedding), while SpeechBrain's ECAPA recipe reports 0.90% EER without score normalization and 0.80% with s-norm on VoxCeleb (https://github.com/speechbrain/speechbrain/blob/develop/recipes/VoxCeleb/SpeakerRec/README.md). Those numbers are benchmark-specific, not product thresholds.

2. Multi-sample profiles. Centroid averaging is the standard baseline: GE2E defines the speaker voiceprint as the centroid of enrollment embeddings and scores with cosine similarity (https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.10467). It is cheap and robust to ordinary within-speaker variation, but it can blur distinct modes such as headset, laptop mic, phone call, and compressed meeting audio. Keeping K reference embeddings preserves those modes: score against each reference and use max, top-k mean, or condition-matched mean. This costs little locally and gives better diagnostics, but one mislabeled or noisy sample becomes a permanent false-accept attractor. Adaptive EMA updates should be gated behind user confirmation or very high confidence plus strong margin; otherwise they drift toward frequent confusions and can be poisoned by a bad diarization cluster. Keep raw references, a recomputable centroid, timestamps/device tags, and rollback.

3. Minimum speech duration. Short utterances are a known failure mode: a short-utterance paper calls duration <=2s one of the main threats to verification and shows only partial recovery with compensation (https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.10884). pyannote's embedding example uses a 3s sliding window for embedding extraction (https://huggingface.co/pyannote/embedding); Resemblyzer's practitioner docs recommend a profile from 5-30s of speech (https://github.com/resemble-ai/Resemblyzer); pyannote voiceprints require a single speaker and cap samples at 30s (https://docs.pyannote.ai/tutorials/identification-with-voiceprints). For meeting UX, do not embed "yeah", "right", coughs, laughter, or overlaps as identity evidence. A snap-to-surrounding-turn heuristic is reasonable: if a short backchannel is between nearby turns from the same confidently named diarization speaker, inherit that display name. Alternatives are cluster-level scoring over accumulated speech, temporal smoothing/Viterbi over labels, or leaving the segment generic.

4. Channel mismatch. Channel mismatch is not an edge case; it is the default in meetings. The Far-Field Speaker Verification Challenge enrolled with close-talk cellphone audio and tested on far-field microphone arrays; even a cosine-scored baseline had EERs around 6.27-7.18% (https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.08046). ECAPA diarization work improves robustness with augmentation and reports strong behavior under close- and distant-talking conditions, which is a clue that training and calibration conditions matter (https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.01466). Mitigations: store per-device/per-audio-path references, prefer same-condition references at scoring time, reject low-SNR/overlapped segments, calibrate thresholds per path, and consider AS-norm/cohort score normalization once there are enough local impostor/reference embeddings; AS-norm explicitly normalizes scores using similar impostor cohorts (https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.04512).

5. Open-set policy and UX tiers. Nearest-neighbor assignment alone is closed-set behavior and will name strangers. Require top1 >= threshold and top1 - top2 >= margin; GE2E's contrast loss formalizes the same "true centroid vs hardest negative" pressure during training (https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.10467). pyannote's shipped API exposes identification confidence scores, matching thresholds, and exclusive matching, and recommends human review for low-confidence segments (https://docs.pyannote.ai/tutorials/confidence-scores). Product tiers should be honest: high confidence auto-labels; medium confidence shows a tentative label or review affordance; low confidence remains "Unknown speaker".

Recommended defaults:

- Threshold: collect local same-app trials immediately; before calibration, use cosine tau=0.75 as a conservative auto-label cutoff and test 0.65-0.80 in 0.05 steps. If using pyannote-style 0-100 confidence, start auto-label at 70 and tentative at 50-69.
- Margin: require top1 - top2 >= 0.08 cosine for auto-label; 0.04-0.08 is tentative; below that stays unknown.
- Minimum duration: embed only clean, non-overlapped turns >=3s; build a profile from at least 15-30s total speech across 3+ turns, with no single enrollment clip over 30s.
- Short backchannels: never update profiles from <2s clips; display-name them only by temporal/diarization context, otherwise leave generic.
- Profile update rule: keep K=5-10 L2-normalized reference embeddings plus a recomputed centroid; add samples only after explicit user confirmation or high-confidence review, never from silent EMA alone.
- Unknown/channel policy: keep separate condition tags or subprofiles for headset, laptop/room mic, and meeting-app audio; require threshold + margin, and prefer "Unknown" over a low-margin known-speaker guess.
