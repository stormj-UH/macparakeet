# Cross-Recording Speaker Identification Prior Art

Bottom line: I found two products that publicly document persistent cross-recording voice identity: Otter.ai and Circleback. Most others ship per-recording diarization, participant-name inference, vocabulary, or no speaker feature.

## Findings

**MacWhisper — no.** MacWhisper 12 supports automatic speaker recognition for compatible local WhisperKit models and ElevenLabs/Deepgram cloud providers, but it is per-transcript diarization: it creates `Speaker 1`, `Speaker 2`, etc., then lets users rename, merge split speakers, adjust segments, and autofill names from a common-speaker-name list. That name reuse is not documented as future voice matching. Official caveat: the same person can be split into multiple speakers and needs manual merge. Sources: https://macwhisper.helpscoutdocs.com/article/32-automatic-speaker-recognition-in-macwhisper and https://www.macwhisper.com/.

**Otter.ai — yes.** Enrollment is correction-based: tagging a speaker teaches Otter to recognize that voice in future conversations; no separate voice-sample enrollment was found. Otter uses account speakers plus Workspace/Channel shared speakers after recording/import processing. Unknowns are handled as `Speaker #` when Otter found a unique but unprofiled speaker, or `unknown speaker` when it cannot identify a unique speaker; `Rematch` can apply newer profiles to old `Speaker #` clusters, not unknowns. Matching is account/workspace/cloud-side; no on-device claim. Consent language specific to enrolling other people's voices was not found; broader consent risk exists around recording/privacy. Accuracy complaints: the docs expect retagging, untagging, support escalation, and weak help-article feedback. Sources: https://help.otter.ai/hc/en-us/articles/21665587209367-Speaker-Identification-Overview, https://help.otter.ai/hc/en-us/articles/360048465453-Tagging-speaker-names-in-a-conversation, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otter.ai.

**Granola — no; requested/discussed.** Desktop currently shows `Me` and `Them`; Granola says live desktop diarization is not supported yet. iPhone can label up to 10 in-person speakers as `Speaker A`, `Speaker B`, etc., but that is not persistent identity. Its People/Companies feature is calendar-attendee based, not voice based. Consent framing is strong for transcription generally: ask before transcribing, optionally post an automated chat notice, and/or show a video watermark. Sources: https://docs.granola.ai/help-center/taking-notes/transcription, https://docs.granola.ai/help-center/feature-requests, https://docs.granola.ai/help-center/people-and-companies, https://docs.granola.ai/help-center/consent-security-privacy/getting-consent, https://docs.granola.ai/help-center/consent-security-privacy/transparency-solutions/introduction.

**superwhisper — no.** It supports per-meeting speaker separation via `Identify Speakers`; labels appear in Segments, can be renamed, and can be copied into AI workflows. No enrollment, future auto-labeling, unknown-speaker prompt, or consent flow for saving other people's voices was found. Models can be local or cloud; Nova is recommended for speaker separation quality. Sources: https://superwhisper.com/docs/modes/meeting, https://superwhisper.com/docs/modes/speaker-separated-meetings, https://superwhisper.com/docs/models/voice.

**Fathom — no evidence found.** Fathom documents transcripts, summaries, recordings, Ask Fathom across meetings, bot/bot-free capture, and recording visibility/consent mechanics, but I found no persistent voice-profile or future auto-labeling flow. Sources: https://www.fathom.ai/, https://help.fathom.video/en/categories/72000-faqs, https://help.fathom.video/en/categories/65984-using-fathom-after-a-call.

**Circleback — yes.** Enrollment is correction-based and calendar-scoped: when a participant name is edited in a transcript, Circleback updates the meeting artifacts; for meetings tied to a calendar event, that participant voice is saved and recognized in future meetings where they are invited. Unknown handling is lightly documented: users select a participant name and type/select a replacement. Matching appears account/cloud-side; no on-device claim. Consent framing: bot meetings include a non-disableable chat message covering recording storage and note sharing, while bot-free capture is less explicit in the opened docs. No user accuracy complaints were found in this bounded pass. Sources: https://circleback.ai/, https://support.circleback.ai/en/articles/10755245-update-meeting-participant-names, https://support.circleback.ai/en/articles/11090138-getting-started-with-recording, https://support.circleback.ai/en/articles/10478509-customize-meeting-participant, https://support.circleback.ai/en/articles/10460553-security.

**Krisp — no evidence found.** Krisp transcribes/summarizes online, hybrid, in-person, and uploaded meetings; it supports on-device English transcription, server-based transcription for 15 more languages, and custom vocabulary for names/terms, but I found no persistent speaker-profile feature. Source: https://krisp.ai/.

**VoiceInk — no.** VoiceInk is local/offline dictation, not meeting diarization. It has a personal dictionary for names/terms and optional cloud text enhancement, but no speaker separation or voice-profile enrollment. Sources: https://tryvoiceink.com/ and https://github.com/Beingpax/VoiceInk.

**Apple Voice Memos / Notes call transcription — no cross-recording profiles.** Voice Memos and Notes can transcribe recordings. Phone call transcription identifies the speaker of each statement within a recorded call, plays an audio notice to both participants, and warns users to verify transcript accuracy, but I found no reusable voice profiles or future auto-labeling. Sources: https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/view-a-transcription-iph00953a982/ios, https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/record-and-transcribe-audio-iphbe11247b5/ios, https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/record-and-transcribe-a-call-iph57c6590e9/ios.

## Patterns Worth Copying / Avoiding

- Copy correction-based enrollment: tag once, improve future labeling, avoid making users record artificial samples.
- Copy scoped identity sharing: account, workspace/channel, or calendar-invite boundaries are easier to explain than global voice memory.
- Copy distinct unknown states: separate "new unique speaker" from "unmatched/unknown," and support rematching old clusters.
- Avoid silent enrollment of other people's voices; say who is enrolled, who can reuse the profile, how to delete it, and what consent is expected.
- Avoid marketing per-recording diarization as identity; persistent profiles need visible confidence, correction, undo, and audit controls.
