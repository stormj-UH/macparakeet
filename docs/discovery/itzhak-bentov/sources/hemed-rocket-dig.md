# HEMED / "Israel's first rocket" dig

Goal: test whether the Bentov/HEMED rocket claim can be moved beyond memoir and downstream summaries toward primary or archival corroboration.

## Bottom line

The claim moved **slightly** but not decisively. This pass found better support for the institutional setting: HEMED / the Israeli Science Corps existed, was formed around 1947-1948, did weapons R&D, worked on rockets/missiles at Rehovot/Rafael-lineage sites, and later fed RAFAEL and other Israeli defense-science institutions. It also found a direct Sharona Muir interview statement that her memoir arose from learning that her father had built Israel's first rocket with a secret group of scientists. But it did **not** find a primary roster, archive record, technical report, personnel file, or contemporary 1948 document naming Bentov on a rocket program.

Reliability grade for the Bentov-specific rocket claim remains: **C / memoir-dependent, historically plausible, not independently proven in this pass.**

## What improved

### HEMED as a real institutional context

- Avner Cohen's *Nonproliferation Review* article, "Israel and Chemical/Biological Weapons: History, Deterrence, and Arms Control," describes a small Haganah scientific department in the mid-1940s and says a Science Corps known as HEMED was established within the IDF in 1948, with Ephraim Katachalsky/Katzir appointed commander after his return in late May 1948. Source: https://www.nonproliferation.org/wp-content/uploads/npr/83cohen.pdf
- Hebrew Wikipedia's "חיל המדע" page is not primary, but it usefully points to the primary/near-primary bibliography: Gideon Yekutieli's *ימי חמ"ד* (Weizmann Institute), Ephraim Katzir's memoir, Uriel Bachrach's *בכוח הידע*, and Ari Barell's article on Ben-Gurion and the founding of HEMED. It also summarizes the unit as a science/engineering R&D and weapons-production unit active from 1948 to 1952. Source: https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D7%97%D7%99%D7%9C_%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%93%D7%A2
- The Davidson Institute/Ynet article "ההיסטוריה של חיל המדע" says HEMED developed and produced weapons during the War of Independence and laid the foundation for Israeli defense industry bodies such as Rafael. It is secondary journalism, but by a science-history writer/editor and grounded in the HEMED literature. Source: https://www.ynet.co.il/articles/0,7340,L-5233824,00.html
- Hayadan's review/summary of Uriel Bachrach's HEMED book says Ben-Gurion pushed Aharon Katzir to recruit young scientists for weapon development, Ephraim Katzir later returned to command the Science Corps, and HEMED developed grenades, mines, incendiaries, mortars, and other improvised weapons under severe constraints. It also mentions later missile/rocket work in the HEMED/RAFAEL lineage, but does not name Bentov. Source: https://www.hayadan.org.il/israeli-science-army-1406096

### Muir / memoir trail is stronger than anonymous retellings

- Google Books and WorldCat both summarize *The Book of Telling* as saying Muir learned after Bentov's death that he created or invented Israel's first rocket and was part of the secret Science Corps/Hemmed. Sources: https://books.google.com/books/about/The_Book_of_Telling.html?id=sflhMx9rNTcC and https://search.worldcat.org/title/57039257?tab=details
- Jewish Book Council's review says Muir learned one of her father's important inventions was Israel's first rocket, that he was a member of Hemmed, and that she attended a 1999 reunion of surviving members who described her father. This is still a review, not the underlying archive, but it adds a specific route by which the memoir may have been sourced: surviving-member testimony. Source: https://www.jewishbookcouncil.org/book/the-book-of-telling-tracing-the-secret-of-my-fathers-lives
- A later interview/Q&A with Sharona Muir about *Invisible Beasts* states that *The Book of Telling* arose from her chance discovery that her father had built Israel's first rocket with a secret group of scientists during the War of Independence. This is direct author testimony, but still not primary technical documentation. Source mirror: https://zorosko.blogspot.com/2014/11/sharona-muir-imaginative-delightful.html
- University of Nebraska Press's 2008 blog post points to author readings from *The Book of Telling*, including readings described as about "Israel's first rocket." This confirms that the rocket material is central in the book's public presentation, but the video/page-level content was not retrieved as a transcript in this pass. Source: https://unpblog.com/2008/02/20/readings-from-t/

## What did not move

- No HEMED personnel list naming "Itzhak Bentov," "Itzhak Ben-Tov," "Emerich Tobias," "Imre Tobias," or Hebrew "יצחק בנטוב" was found.
- Hebrew searches for "יצחק בנטוב" with "חמד", "חיל המדע", and "רקטה" mostly returned downstream biography summaries rather than archival records.
- Searches around early Israeli rocketry surfaced better-documented later missile/rocket programs such as Luz/Shavit 2, with named figures such as Moshe Epstein, Yonatan Mass, Anselm Yaron, and Uzi Sharon. Those are valuable context for Israeli rocketry but do not corroborate Bentov's 1948 role.
- The "first rocket" wording may be ambiguous. It could mean a wartime improvised rocket/rocket-assisted weapon, not the later public "first Israeli rocket/space" claims around Shavit 2 or the guided Luz missile. The sources found here do not identify the device, test date, site, range, payload, unit, or team roster.

## What would settle it

The claim would move from memoir-dependent to well documented if any one of these turned up:

- A HEMED / IDF / Haganah archival personnel file or roster naming Bentov under the relevant Science Corps group.
- A 1948-1952 HEMED technical report, test log, procurement document, photograph caption, or project file naming Bentov and describing the rocket/weapon.
- A page-level citation from Muir's memoir naming her specific informants, reunion participants, documents, or archive call numbers for Bentov's HEMED work.
- A corroborating memoir/oral-history passage from another HEMED member naming Bentov on the rocket project.
- A declassified RAFAEL/EMET/HEMED institutional history that lists early rocket personnel and includes Bentov.
