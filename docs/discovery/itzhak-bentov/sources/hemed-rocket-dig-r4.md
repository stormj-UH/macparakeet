# HEMED / "Israel's first rocket" dig - round 4

Question: can the claim that Itzhak Bentov designed or built Israel's first
rocket in the 1948 war be corroborated from Hebrew, institutional, or archival
sources independent of Sharona Ben-Tov Muir's memoir?

## Verdict

The claim remains **single-family-sourced for Bentov specifically**.

This round strengthened the surrounding institutional picture: HEMED / חיל המדע
was real; it was created out of the Haganah/IDF wartime science effort; it did
weapons R&D; and the Rehovot base is described in Hebrew and participant sources
as doing work on **missiles, rockets, radio guidance, and rocket propulsion**.
Those sources name documented HEMED figures such as the Katzir brothers, Ernst
David Bergmann, Yohanan Ratner, Meir Rabinowitch, Saul Amarel, Meir Birk, Abraham
Kogan, Ephraim Frei, the Racah student group, Amos de-Shalit, Yigal Talmi,
Gideon Yekutieli, Gavriel Goldring, Uri Haber-Schaim, Israel Pelah, Israel
Navot, and Moshe "Moya" Epstein.

But no retrieved roster, archive record, HEMED history, Israeli defense-history
paper, obituary, or memoir by another HEMED member independently names Bentov on
the rocket project. The only retrieved sources that name Bentov as the rocket
inventor trace back to Muir's memoir, reviews of it, publisher descriptions, or
modern unsourced profiles.

Reliability grade after round 4:
**C+ for historical plausibility; C for Bentov-specific proof.**

## What moved

### Saul Amarel memoir sketch: strongest adjacent HEMED rocket/missile source

- URL: https://studylib.net/doc/8987563/sketch-of-war-memoirs-%E2%80%93-from-1940-to-1952
- Source: Saul Amarel, "War Memoirs Sketch: Greece & Palestine, 1940-1952,"
  running draft dated November 20, 1999.
- Relevant text: Amarel says that in March 1948 he was asked to join a special
  unit called Hemed / Heil Madah. He lists early Technion and Hebrew University
  HEMED people, including Meir Birk, Moya, Israel Falchovits, Gideon Yekutieli,
  Yigal Talmi, Amos de-Shalit, and Abraham Kogan. He says Kogan did key research
  in rocket propulsion at HEMED.
- More rocket/missile context: Amarel calls the Land Kelev projects early
  pioneering projects in Israeli missile development, describes HEMED work at
  Rehovot, and later lists "Abraham Kogan et al on rocketry and solid state
  rocket propellants."
- Reliability grade: **B.** This is a participant memoir draft hosted on a
  mirror, not an archive scan, but it is highly relevant because Amarel describes
  his own HEMED work and names people/projects in the same Rehovot/Weizmann
  circle.
- Bentov named? **No.** Searches for Bentov / Ben-Tov / בנטוב on the page
  returned no match.
- Independent of memoir? Yes for early HEMED missile/rocket work; no for Bentov.
- Assessment: strong adjacent corroboration and meaningful negative evidence.
  It validates that early HEMED rocket/missile work existed and identifies other
  personnel, but it does not corroborate Bentov. Its lists contain "others?"
  caveats, so absence is not dispositive.

### Hebrew Wikipedia: direct claim, but unsourced

- URL: https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D7%99%D7%A6%D7%97%D7%A7_%D7%91%D7%A0%D7%98%D7%95%D7%91
- Exact Hebrew text: "במלחמת העצמאות, במסגרת שרותו בחיל המדע, תכנן את הטיל
  הראשון של מדינת ישראל."
- Translation: "In the War of Independence, as part of his service in the
  Science Corps, [he] designed the first missile/rocket of the State of Israel."
- Reliability grade: **C-.** It is a direct Hebrew statement, but the page has no
  inline citation for the claim. It is therefore useful as a search lead, not
  independent corroboration.
- Bentov named? Yes.
- Independent of memoir? Not established; likely downstream of the same memoir
  or modern English summaries unless proven otherwise.

### Hebrew HEMED article: rockets/missiles at Rehovot, but no Bentov

- URL: https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D7%97%D7%99%D7%9C_%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%93%D7%A2
- Exact Hebrew text: "בבסיס היחידה ברחובות ... נעשה מחקר בפיתוח טילים ורקטות."
- Translation: "At the unit's Rehovot base ... research was conducted in the
  development of missiles and rockets."
- Additional named context: the same section names Ephraim Frei for the radio
  guided "כלב" explosive cart, lists the Racah student group, and says a Technion
  student group at Rehovot was led by Moshe "Moya" Epstein.
- Reliability grade: **B- for institutional/technical context, C for individual
  attribution.** Wikipedia is secondary, but it points to the standard HEMED
  literature and gives a concrete rockets/missiles context.
- Bentov named? No.
- Independent of memoir? Yes for HEMED rocket/missile work generally; no for
  Bentov.

### Ari Barell, Tel Aviv University PDF: HEMED founding, no Bentov hit

- URL: https://mideast-africa.m.tau.ac.il/sites/humanities.tau.ac.il/files/media_server/humanities/zionism/%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%90%D7%9C/15/Israel15_barel.pdf
- Article: Ari Barell, "המנהיג, המדענים והמלחמה: דוד בן-גוריון והקמת חיל המדע,"
  *Israel* 15 (2009), pp. 67-92.
- Relevant text found by local PDF extraction: the article states that in March
  1948 the Science Corps was established, that it grew into a major research
  institution, and that it became the basis for later RAFAEL.
- Search result: local text search for בנטוב / Bentov found no hit.
- Reliability grade: **A- for HEMED institutional founding; negative for Bentov
  attribution.**
- Bentov named? No.
- Independent of memoir? Yes, but it does not corroborate Bentov.

### Ben-Gurion Archive diary: primary institutional context, no Bentov

- URL: https://bengurionarchive.bgu.ac.il/node/12904
- Source: David Ben-Gurion diary, March 28, 1950.
- Relevant Hebrew text: "חמד יעשה רק עבודות מחקר, אולם לא עבודות פיתוח טכנולוגי
  של נשק."
- Translation: "HEMED will do only research work, not technological development
  of weapons."
- Context: the diary records a live dispute over weapon production/development
  boundaries among HEMED, military industry, and other bodies.
- Reliability grade: **A- for primary institutional context; not a Bentov
  source.**
- Bentov named? No.
- Independent of memoir? Yes, but it does not corroborate Bentov.

### RAFAEL official history: Moya Epstein, not Bentov

- URL: https://he.rafael.co.il/history/%D7%9C%D7%96%D7%9B%D7%A8-%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%99%D7%94/
- Source: RAFAEL official history/memorial page, "לזכר מויה."
- Relevant Hebrew text: the page describes Dr. Moshe "Moya" Epstein as "מדור
  המייסדים של חיל המדע" and "בכיר מפתחי הטילים ברפאל."
- Translation: Epstein was from the founding generation of the Science Corps and
  a senior developer of missiles at RAFAEL.
- Additional context: the page says that in 1948 Epstein designed an early
  recoilless-cannon model, that in 1956 he developed the Luz, and that he later
  led early chaff-rocket work.
- Reliability grade: **B for RAFAEL-lineage institutional history.**
- Bentov named? No.
- Independent of memoir? Yes, but it points to other named missile/rocket
  figures, not Bentov.

### Google Books: Muir source genealogy, more specific but still memoir

- URL: https://books.google.com/books/about/The_Book_of_Telling.html?id=mE0qAQAAIAAJ
- Exact accessible description: after her father died, Muir learned he had
  "invented Israel's first rocket"; she later learned he belonged to a top-secret
  group called Hemmed and traveled to Israel to meet his colleagues.
- More specific setting: the description says the group worked in a rooftop shed
  in Tel Aviv with the equivalent of $3,000.
- Reliability grade: **B for what the book/publisher says; C for independent
  historical proof.**
- Bentov named? Yes.
- Independent of memoir? No.

### Jewish Book Council review: reunion detail, still downstream of Muir

- URL: https://www.jewishbookcouncil.org/book/the-book-of-telling-tracing-the-secret-of-my-fathers-lives
- Relevant text: JBC says Muir learned one of her father's important inventions
  was Israel's first rocket; that Itzhak Bentov was a member of Hemmed; and that
  in 1999 she attended a reunion of surviving members who described her father.
- Reliability grade: **B- for review summary of the memoir; C+ for source-path
  detail.** The reunion detail is useful because it explains how Muir may have
  gathered testimony, but it is not a transcript or independent witness record.
- Bentov named? Yes.
- Independent of memoir? No.

### J. / Jewish News of Northern California review: surviving-member route

- URL: https://jweekly.com/2005/10/07/bias-good-prose-butt-heads-in-tale-of-israel-s-manhattan-project/
- Relevant text: the review says Bentov invented Israel's first rocket in 1948
  and that Muir interviewed surviving Hemmed members; it also says much of the
  Hemmed experience remained withheld from her for security reasons.
- Reliability grade: **B- for reporting what the memoir says and noting
  interviews; C for independent proof.**
- Bentov named? Yes.
- Independent of memoir? No. This is a review of Muir's book.

## What did not move

- No HEMED roster naming Bentov / Ben-Tov / יצחק בנטוב was retrieved.
- No Haganah, IDF, RAFAEL, Weizmann, Ben-Gurion Archive, or Israeli State Archive
  technical file naming Bentov was retrieved.
- No independent HEMED member memoir or oral history naming Bentov on the rocket
  project was retrieved.
- No contemporary 1948-1954 Israeli newspaper item naming Bentov in HEMED or
  rocket work was retrieved.
- Hebrew searches for יצחק בנטוב + חמד / חיל המדע / רקטה / טיל mostly returned
  Hebrew Wikipedia, modern popular summaries, religious/popular profiles, or
  downstream English-derived summaries.
- Searches on alternate names, including Emerich Tobias / Imre Tobias, did not
  surface an archival HEMED hit.

## Source-honest reading

The current source stack supports this wording:

> Sharona Ben-Tov Muir's memoir and its reviews say Bentov created Israel's first
> rocket as part of HEMED. HEMED itself and wartime rocket/missile work are
> independently documented, including Hebrew accounts of rocket/missile research
> at Rehovot. This round did not find an independent archival or member source
> naming Bentov on that work.

It does **not** support this stronger wording:

> Bentov is independently documented by Israeli defense-history sources as the
> designer of Israel's first rocket.

## Exact primary document that would settle it

The cleanest settling document would be a **1948-1952 HEMED / Haganah / IDF /
RAFAEL project file, personnel roster, test log, photograph caption, or technical
report** naming **יצחק בנטוב / Itzhak Bentov / Itzhak Ben-Tov / Imre Tobias** and
linking him to the named rocket or missile project.

A second-best settling source would be a transcript or archive recording from
the 1999 Hemmed reunion, or another HEMED member's memoir/oral history, naming
Bentov and describing the device, site, test, team, or archive call number.
