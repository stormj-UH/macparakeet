# Flight 191 / Bentov primary-era check

Question: can the repeated claim that Itzhak Bentov died on American Airlines
Flight 191 on May 25, 1979 be moved from modern repetition to a primary-era
source?

## Bottom line

Yes, but with a naming caveat. This round found contemporary passenger-list
reporting from May 27, 1979 that names a Flight 191 passenger as **"BENTOV, I.,
Weston, Mass."** / **"Bentov, I.-Weston, Mass."** The New York Times archive
page says the list was released by American Airlines and provided by the
Associated Press. The Washington Post carried the same abbreviated listing.

That is strong primary-era confirmation that a Massachusetts passenger listed as
I. Bentov was aboard Flight 191. The remaining identity bridge is that the
retrieved 1979 list abbreviates the first name. A modern Des Plaines memorial
program gives the full name **"Bentov, Itzhak"**, and Marilyn Bentov's 1981
first-person essay says her former husband Itzhak Bentov was one of those killed.
Together those make the identification very strong, but they still do not replace
the missing full-name airline manifest, coroner record, or death certificate.

Reliability grade for the death-on-Flight-191 claim after this round:
**A- for passenger-status confirmation; B+ for full-name identity resolution.**

## Itinerary: California / Japanese scientists

The narrow "to California" part is consistent with the primary NTSB route:
Flight 191 was bound from Chicago O'Hare to Los Angeles.

The more specific "to meet or present to Japanese scientists" detail did **not**
move. It appears in modern secondary/popular accounts and Wikipedia-derived
text, but this round found no contemporary obituary, conference notice,
invitation, program, family paper, airline itinerary, or page-level memoir
excerpt that says Bentov was traveling to meet Japanese scientists.

Reliability grade for the Japanese-scientists itinerary: **C- / unproven**.

## Dead ends and access limits

- NYT live archive: title/record reachable, but body text not reachable through
  the live page here; Wayback supplied the readable text.
- Chicago Tribune: modern Flight 191 victim pages are useful, but no accessible
  1979 Tribune Bentov obituary or full-name passenger-list scan was retrieved.
- Boston Globe / Boston-area / Belmont-Weston local papers: targeted web
  searches did not retrieve an accessible May-June 1979 obituary or death
  notice. Geni hints at a Boston Globe record, likely via a genealogy/paywall
  source, but no text was accessible.
- Jewish press: no 1979 Jewish press death notice found. Later Jewish-book
  coverage addresses the memoir/HEMED story, not the Flight 191 passenger list.
- NTSB victim list: the retrieved NTSB sources do not list individual passengers.
- Cook County / Illinois casualty records: no public online primary record
  naming Bentov was retrieved.
- Medical-device trade press: no primary-era industry obituary or notice found.
- Sunday Herald / UPI: Wikipedia cites a May 27, 1979 Sunday Herald article
  titled "Jumbo Jet Crashes On Take Off"; this round did not retrieve the
  article text/page image directly.

## Missing document that would settle it

The single best remaining document is the original **American Airlines passenger
manifest / released casualty list**, or an official **coroner/death-certificate
record**, naming **Itzhak Bentov** or **Itzhak E. Bentov** in full as a Flight
191 decedent. The 1979 NYT/Washington Post list is now enough to retire the
"only downstream of Wikipedia" caveat, but the full-name primary document is
still missing.
