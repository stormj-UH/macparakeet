Itzhak "Ben" Bentov (Aug 9, 1923 – May 25, 1979): self-taught mechanical
engineer who built Israel's first rocket and the steerable cardiac catheter,
co-founded Medi-Tech (→ Boston Scientific lineage), then turned his engineer's
mind to consciousness — modeling the body as a resonant oscillator system.
His work was later cited in the U.S. Army's 1983 "Analysis and Assessment of
Gateway Process" report (declassified by the CIA in 2003). He died on American
Airlines Flight 191.
